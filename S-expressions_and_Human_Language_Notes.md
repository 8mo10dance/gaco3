# Thoughts on Programming Languages and Human Languages

## My Observation

I started with a simple question:

> Why do S-expressions feel so natural to me, even when I am trying to
> learn English?

At first, I thought the answer was about syntax. Then I realized it was
about **abstraction**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## S-expressions and Ruby-like Syntax

I love S-expressions.

They have a uniform structure.

``` scheme
(map f xs)
(if p a b)
(let ((x 1)) ...)
```

Every expression follows the same rule.

Ruby-like syntax feels closer to English.

``` ruby
user.posts.first.author.name
I.want_to(:learn, :english)
```

It reads almost like natural language.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## A New Distinction

I realized that programming languages and human languages use
abstraction differently.

**Programming languages**

-   Abstraction represents **structure**.
-   Functions, modules, classes, and macros organize computation.
-   Abstraction is an explicit mechanism in the language.

**Human languages**

-   Abstraction represents **concepts**.
-   Words such as *justice*, *freedom*, and *beauty* refer to ideas
    formed in the mind.
-   Abstraction is primarily a cognitive activity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Key Insight

Programming languages encode abstraction.

Human languages express abstraction.

Or, more precisely:

> Programming languages are designed to express computation and
> structure.

> Human languages are designed to express thought.

Their different purposes explain why their abstractions look different.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why Ruby Feels More Natural

Ruby-like syntax follows the pattern of English:

Subject → Verb → Object

Examples:

-   I think.
-   I want to learn English.
-   user.posts.first.author

This makes Ruby-like syntax feel close to natural language.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why I Still Prefer S-expressions

Even if Ruby-like syntax resembles English more closely, I still prefer
S-expressions.

Why?

Because I value **uniformity**.

Every expression has the same shape.

Once my brain stops seeing parentheses, it starts seeing trees.

I do not love S-expressions because they resemble human language.

I love them because they reveal structure directly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## My Current Conclusion

Programming languages and human languages have different purposes.

Programming languages exist to express computation and structure.

Human languages exist to express thought.

S-expressions are excellent for programming because they expose
structure.

Ruby-like syntax is excellent for English because it follows the flow of
natural language.

Both are beautiful.

But if I have to choose, I will always choose S-expressions.
